# Monetization Strategy

## Product Principle

TribeApp should feel useful before it asks users to pay. The free product must allow people to build a real profile, discover relevant people, save profiles, form mutual connections, and message matched users. Premium should make discovery more efficient, give users more control, and add insight, not block the core social experience.

## Recommended Free Plan

The free plan should be generous enough to validate the network and create real social value.

Recommended name: Tribe Free

Free users can:

- Create and edit a profile.
- Complete personality onboarding.
- Upload the required minimum of 3 profile photos.
- Add profile prompts and a voice introduction.
- Appear in recommendations if they meet profile quality requirements.
- View a limited daily set of recommendations.
- Save a limited number of profiles per day.
- Pass profiles.
- Undo the most recent pass.
- Match through mutual saves.
- Message mutual matches.
- Receive notifications.
- Block and report users.
- Use basic discovery filters such as intent, city, and availability.

Suggested free limits:

- Recommendations: 15 per day.
- Saves: 8 per day.
- Matches: unlimited when mutual save happens.
- Messages with mutual matches: unlimited text-only messaging.
- Undo pass: last pass only.
- Advanced filters: locked.

Do not limit basic matching too aggressively. If free users cannot form meaningful connections, the app will feel empty and transactional.

## Recommended Premium Plan

Recommended name: Tribe Plus

Premium should unlock control, visibility, insight, and convenience.

Premium users unlock:

- See who saved you.
- See who liked you.
- Unlimited undo pass.
- Advanced filters.
- Boost visibility.
- Profile analytics.
- Incognito mode.
- Priority placement in compatible recommendation pools.
- Larger daily recommendation allowance.
- Larger daily save allowance.
- AI profile coach later.
- Premium communities later.

Suggested premium limits:

- Recommendations: unlimited or 75 per day during MVP.
- Saves: unlimited or 50 per day during MVP.
- Matches: never hard-limited.
- Messaging: unlimited with mutual matches.
- Advanced filters: intent, city, distance, age range if applicable, availability, interests, personality type, conversation style, profile completeness, verified profiles.

## Pricing Recommendation

Start with one premium tier. Avoid multiple paid tiers until retention, conversion, and user density are proven.

Suggested monthly pricing:

- Tribe Plus Monthly: NGN 2,500 per month.
- Launch promo: NGN 1,500 per month for the first 60 to 90 days.

Suggested yearly pricing:

- Tribe Plus Yearly: NGN 24,000 per year.
- Launch promo: NGN 15,000 to NGN 18,000 per year.

Yearly pricing should give roughly 20 percent savings compared with monthly billing. Keep pricing easy to understand and avoid complex credit systems during MVP.

## Feature Packaging

### Keep Free

These features should remain free so TribeApp still feels useful:

- Account creation.
- Personality onboarding.
- Profile creation and editing.
- Required photo uploads.
- Profile prompts.
- Voice introduction.
- Basic recommendations.
- A reasonable daily save allowance.
- Mutual matching.
- Text messaging with mutual matches.
- Notifications.
- Blocking and reporting.
- Safety controls.
- Basic privacy settings.

### Premium Unlocks

These features are good premium candidates:

- See who saved you.
- See who liked you.
- Unlimited undo pass.
- Advanced filters.
- Boost visibility.
- Profile analytics.
- Incognito mode.
- Verified-only discovery filter.
- More daily recommendations.
- More daily saves.
- AI profile coach later.
- Premium communities later.

### Limit Carefully

Recommendations can be limited for free users, but the limit should not make the app feel unusable. Saves can be limited because they are a high-intent action, but matches should not be limited once they happen. Messaging should remain free after mutual match because communication is the core value of the product.

Recommended limits:

- Limit free recommendations modestly.
- Limit free saves modestly.
- Do not limit mutual matches.
- Do not charge to message mutual matches.
- Do not charge users to appear in the basic recommendation pool.
- Do not hide essential safety tools behind premium.

## What Not To Monetize Too Early

Avoid monetizing these too soon:

- Basic messaging after mutual match.
- Basic profile visibility.
- Account verification.
- Blocking and reporting.
- Safety and moderation controls.
- Required profile quality features.
- Core onboarding.
- Basic discovery access.
- Basic notifications.
- Any feature that makes the app feel safe or trustworthy.

Charging for trust or core connection too early can damage growth, especially before TribeApp has strong user density.

## Recommended Rollout Plan

### Stage 1: MVP Validation

Launch with no paid plan or with a visible "Plus coming soon" preview. Track:

- Completed onboarding rate.
- Profile completion rate.
- Daily active users.
- Recommendation views.
- Save rate.
- Mutual save rate.
- Message start rate.
- Message reply rate.
- Reports and blocks.
- Day 1, Day 7, and Day 30 retention.

### Stage 2: Soft Premium Launch

Introduce Tribe Plus after users are forming real matches. Start with:

- See who saved you.
- Unlimited undo pass.
- Advanced filters.
- More recommendations.
- More saves.

Use launch pricing for early adopters.

### Stage 3: Visibility and Analytics

Add:

- Boost visibility.
- Profile analytics.
- Verified-only filter.
- Incognito mode.

Be careful with boosts. They should improve exposure without making free users invisible.

### Stage 4: Later Premium Expansion

Add only after core retention is healthy:

- AI profile coach.
- Premium communities.
- Deeper profile analytics.
- Event or activity-based premium discovery.

## Risks Of Over-Monetizing

Key risks:

- Users churn if free discovery feels too constrained.
- Users distrust the app if basic visibility feels pay-to-win.
- Network growth slows if free users cannot create enough matches.
- Safety perception suffers if verification or privacy feels like a paid privilege.
- Messaging monetization can make the product feel exploitative.
- Too many premium prompts can make the app feel less calm and personality-first.

Mitigation:

- Keep the core loop free.
- Monetize convenience and insight.
- Use gentle upgrade prompts.
- Avoid aggressive popups.
- Maintain strong safety and privacy features for all users.
- Review conversion and retention together, not conversion alone.

## Best MVP Monetization Approach

The best MVP approach is a freemium model with one paid tier, launched only after the product shows real matching and messaging activity.

Recommended MVP premium bundle:

- See who saved you.
- Unlimited undo pass.
- Advanced filters.
- Higher recommendation limit.
- Higher save limit.

Hold these until later:

- Boost visibility.
- Profile analytics.
- AI profile coach.
- Incognito mode.
- Premium communities.

This keeps the MVP simple, avoids overbuilding payments too early, and lets the product prove that users value personality-first discovery before monetizing deeper tools.

## Clear Recommendation

Launch TribeApp with a strong free plan and a single premium plan: Tribe Plus at NGN 2,500 monthly or NGN 24,000 yearly, with a temporary launch promo around NGN 1,500 monthly. Keep messaging, mutual matches, safety tools, profile creation, onboarding, and basic discovery free. Monetize visibility into demand, control, and convenience first: see who saved you, unlimited undo pass, advanced filters, and higher discovery limits. Delay AI, boosts, analytics, incognito mode, and premium communities until retention and network density are proven.
