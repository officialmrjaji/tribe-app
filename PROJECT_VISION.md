# TribeApp Project Vision

Date: June 23, 2026

## Product Thesis

TribeApp is a personality-first social discovery platform for people who want better ways to find aligned friends, collaborators, communities, and low-pressure plans. The product should prioritize temperament, values, pace, shared interests, and conversation quality over surface-level browsing.

The current codebase expresses this direction through a polished discovery workspace with profile cards, match signals, values, circles, prompt modes, and profile detail panels. The next product step is to turn that prototype into a trustworthy social product with real accounts, persisted profiles, permissioned messaging, privacy controls, and moderation.

## Vision Statement

Help people find the right people by making personality, shared values, and social context easier to express, discover, and act on.

## Target Users

- People seeking deeper friendships or local social circles.
- New movers trying to find compatible communities in a city.
- Creatives, builders, students, founders, and hobbyists seeking aligned collaborators.
- People who dislike swipe-heavy social apps but still want discovery.
- Community hosts who want to form small, intentional groups.

## Core User Promise

TribeApp should make users feel:

- Seen beyond demographics and photos.
- In control of privacy, availability, and social pace.
- Safer because conversations and discovery have boundaries.
- More confident starting conversations because prompts and context are built in.
- Less overwhelmed because recommendations are curated and explainable.

## Product Principles

- Personality before popularity.
- Context before cold outreach.
- Permission before messaging.
- Privacy before growth.
- Quality matches before infinite feed volume.
- Explainable recommendations before opaque ranking.
- Small social rituals before performative networking.

## Current Product Shape

The implemented prototype already communicates:

- A discovery dashboard rather than a marketing landing page.
- A profile model built around traits, values, pace, availability, circles, and prompts.
- A right-side detail panel for evaluating compatibility.
- Filters and prompt modes as early interaction patterns.
- A calmer, more intentional interface than swipe-first apps.

## MVP Product Definition

The MVP should prove one core loop:

1. A user creates an account.
2. A user completes a personality-first profile.
3. The system recommends compatible people.
4. The user can save, pass, or request an intro.
5. A permissioned conversation can begin.
6. The user can block/report and control discoverability.

Anything outside that loop is secondary until the first beta community shows retention and safe conversation behavior.

## MVP Differentiators

- Onboarding asks about values, pace, social energy, and desired rituals.
- Discovery explains why a person is recommended.
- Messaging is permissioned and prompt-guided.
- Circles are used as context, not just groups.
- Users can tune discovery by social intent, not only by demographics.

## Non-Goals For MVP

- Public social feed.
- Infinite swipe mechanics.
- Paid subscriptions.
- Creator monetization.
- Large group chat.
- Full event ticketing.
- Complex AI agent behavior.
- Advanced media sharing.
- Multi-city launch at scale.

## Success Metrics

Activation:

- Signup to completed profile conversion.
- Completed profile to first recommendation viewed.
- First recommendation to save/pass action.

Match quality:

- Save rate per recommendation.
- Intro request acceptance rate.
- Conversation start rate.
- First conversation response rate.

Retention:

- Weekly active users in beta.
- Return rate after first conversation.
- Repeat discovery sessions per user.

Safety:

- Reports per active user.
- Blocks per conversation started.
- Moderator response time.
- Percentage of flagged content reviewed.

Trust:

- Profile completion rate.
- Privacy settings usage.
- User-reported match quality.

## Brand Positioning

TribeApp should feel calm, discerning, human, and practical. The brand should avoid both hyper-polished dating-app energy and sterile professional networking language. It should speak like a thoughtful host: clear, warm, and specific.

## Product North Star

The north star metric should be meaningful accepted intros per active user per week, guarded by safety and retention metrics. A "meaningful accepted intro" requires both users to consent to a conversation and at least one reply after the opening message.
